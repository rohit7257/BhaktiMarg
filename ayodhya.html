Ram Mandir

The Ram Mandir, or Ram Temple, is a Hindu temple located in Ayodhya, Uttar Pradesh, India. It is believed by many Hindus to be the birthplace of Lord Rama, a principal deity in Hinduism. The temple is currently under construction, with the main structure completed and consecrated on January 22, 2024.

The temple's construction has a complex history, involving legal disputes and communal tensions. The site was previously home to the Babri Masjid mosque, built in the 16th century. In 1992, the mosque was demolished, leading to significant controversy and legal battles. In 2019, the Supreme Court of India ruled that the land should be given to Hindus for the construction of a temple, while Muslims were allocated land elsewhere in Ayodhya for a mosque.

Construction of the Ram Mandir began with a groundbreaking ceremony on August 5, 2020, led by Prime Minister Narendra Modi. The temple is designed in the Nagara architectural style, featuring intricate carvings and sculptures. The main structure stands 161 feet tall, with the temple complex covering an area of 2.7 acres.

The consecration ceremony on January 22, 2024, marked a significant milestone, attracting a large number of devotees. Following the event, the temple saw a surge in visitors, with over half a million people attending on the first day. As of February 2024, daily visitors numbered between 100,000 and 150,000. The Ram Mandir stands as a symbol of India's rich cultural and religious heritage, drawing devotees and tourists from around the world.
